# Product Vision & Research

## Why We Chose This Problem

Before deciding what to build, we conducted market research, competitor analysis, customer discovery, and customer journey mapping to understand the real operational challenges faced by Pakistani e-commerce businesses.

Initially, our research focused on reducing Return-to-Origin (RTO) losses through automated WhatsApp Cash-on-Delivery (COD) confirmation. However, after analyzing seller workflows, existing support patterns, automation platforms, and the complete post-purchase lifecycle, we realized that COD confirmation is only one part of a much larger operational problem.

---

## Research Process

Our decision was based on four research activities:

1. **Market Research**  
   We studied Pakistani e-commerce businesses, especially Instagram and WhatsApp-based sellers, to understand how they manage orders and customer communication.

2. **Competitor Analysis**  
   We analyzed 10+ local and international customer support and WhatsApp automation platforms, including WATI, Respond.io, ManyChat, Interakt, WeTarseel, and other automation tools.

3. **Customer Discovery**  
   We reached out to Pakistani online sellers and clothing brands to understand their real workflows, repeated customer questions, COD confirmation process, and post-purchase support problems.

4. **Customer Journey Mapping**  
   We mapped the complete customer journey from order placement to delivery, failed delivery handling, exchanges, returns, reviews, and follow-ups.

---

## Key Findings

Our research showed that store owners spend hours every day handling repetitive operational tasks such as:

- Answering repeated customer questions
- Confirming orders
- Verifying addresses
- Sharing tracking details
- Handling delivery failures
- Managing exchanges
- Collecting reviews
- Following up with customers

These tasks consume valuable time, require additional staff, and directly affect revenue, customer satisfaction, and operational efficiency.

---

## Competitor Gap

Our competitor analysis showed that existing platforms provide automation, but they are not designed specifically for Pakistani e-commerce operations.

Most existing solutions:

- Are generic workflow automation tools
- Require businesses to build their own automation flows
- Are priced in USD
- Provide limited Urdu and Roman Urdu support
- Do not fully understand Pakistan's COD-heavy e-commerce workflow
- Focus more on messaging automation than complete post-purchase operations

---

## Product Pivot

This research led us to rethink our product direction.

Instead of building another WhatsApp chatbot or a simple COD confirmation tool, we decided to build **the Product**.

The Product is an AI-powered post-purchase operations agent designed specifically for Pakistani e-commerce brands.

It acts like a virtual operations employee that manages the customer journey automatically while knowing when human intervention is required.

---

|---|---|

| Bigger Problem Found | Complete post-purchase operations are manual and repetitive |

---

## Conclusion

We chose this product because our research showed that Pakistani e-commerce businesses need more than a basic chatbot or COD confirmation tool.

They need an intelligent operations assistant that can manage customer communication, order confirmation, address verification, tracking updates, delivery issues, exchanges, reviews, and follow-ups in a way that fits local business workflows.

This became the foundation for **the Product**.
