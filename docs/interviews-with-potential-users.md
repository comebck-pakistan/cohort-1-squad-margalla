# Interview Insights

## Interview 1 — Footwear Seller (Imported Bulk)

**Date conducted:** ~5 days ago
**Business type:** Footwear (imported bulk)
**Sales channels:** WhatsApp (primary), TikTok (discovery), Instagram (catalogue/credibility), local walk-ins

---

### Business Overview

- Sells imported bulk footwear.
- Customers currently come from local walk-ins and WhatsApp referrals.
- TikTok is being built as the main acquisition channel; Instagram is used for catalogue and credibility.
- Goal: pull online traffic into both in-shop visits and direct WhatsApp orders.

### Sales Channel Breakdown

- **WhatsApp** — the actual conversion channel. This is where orders are closed.
- **TikTok** — drives discovery (comments/DMs funnel into WhatsApp).
- **Instagram** — used as a catalogue, not a sales channel.
- **Daraz** — considered, but rejected. Commission eats margins and the seller loses the direct customer relationship.
- **Shopify** — on the roadmap, but only once order volume justifies it (target: 150+ orders/month consistently).

### Order Volume

- 40–80 orders/month combined (walk-in + WhatsApp), early stage.
- Deliberately growing — prioritizing solid unit economics over chasing volume.

### Current Order Workflow (fully manual)

1. TikTok comment or DM comes in
2. Pushed to WhatsApp
3. Manually check stock
4. Quote price
5. Confirm size
6. Take address
7. Arrange courier or pickup

No automation, no CRM, no saved quick replies at any step.

### Repetitive Questions Answered Daily

- Price
- Size availability
- "COD hai?"
- "Karachi se aata hai kya?" (customers assume local-only sourcing after seeing TikTok content)

### Biggest Time Waster

Manual WhatsApp replies with no system behind them — every customer conversation starts from zero. No pinned catalogue, no auto-reply, no message templates. Seller describes themselves as "a human chatbot answering the same things on loop."

### Causes of Lost Orders / Cancellations / Returns

1. **COD no-shows** (biggest issue) — customer confirms via WhatsApp, courier is dispatched, phone goes off. Shipping cost lost both ways.
2. **Size mismatches** — customer orders a 42, actually wears a 43.
3. **Stock sync issues** — item sold in-shop while an online order for the same item is still open, resulting in disappointed customers.

### COD Confirmation & Address Verification (current process)

- Voice call before dispatch.
- If no pickup after two attempts, order is held.
- No formal flow — purely experience-based judgment.
- Seller's own suggestion: a structured confirmation message with a deadline (e.g. "Confirm in 2 hours or order is released") would scale better than manual calls.

### Order Tracking / "Where is my order?" Handling

- Tracked manually via courier (TCS, Leopards, or M&P depending on city), then relayed to the customer over WhatsApp.
- No tracking link is proactively shared.
- Seller frames this as a **trust and retention problem**, not just an operations problem — customers who get proactive updates don't churn; customers who have to chase updates do.

### Top Task the Seller Wants Eliminated

WhatsApp product inquiries. A simple, clean catalogue (even just a PDF or WhatsApp Catalogue) with sizes, prices, and photos could cut ~60% of inbound messages before they even start — because most inquiries wouldn't exist if the price/size/photo were already visible.

---

## Key Takeaways for Product Direction

- COD confirmation, size mismatches, and stock sync are the three biggest sources of lost revenue — validates our post-purchase-operations focus over a pure chatbot approach.
- Sellers want **proactive** communication (tracking updates, confirmation deadlines), not just reactive replies — this maps directly to an automated ops agent rather than a simple FAQ bot.
- A structured, deadline-based COD confirmation flow was independently suggested by the seller — strong signal this is a real, felt need, not just our assumption.
- Repetitive pre-sale questions (price/size/COD/location) could be reduced with a static catalogue, but post-sale operations (confirmation, tracking, no-shows) remain the harder, unsolved problem — this is where the Product's real value sits.
